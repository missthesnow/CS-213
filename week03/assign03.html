Excerpt from Five Fifths of the World

At that moment, I felt my physical senses leave me. Not in the same way that the fairy had taken them, instantly and cruelly. No, this was fading and dizzying, like I had the chance to reach out and pull them back if I tried. He was not mine to love; I would lose my senses forever.

The smell of the grass and rain fades and with it, the emeralds and cobalts and other nuances of color I had perhaps begun to take for granted. The sound of the pounding rain resigns to my imagination and so does the feel of Damick's hand. I see his mouth move and expect to hear nothing, but I am granted his last words to me.
