Excerpt from Five Fifths of the World

We sat in silence for quite a while. The sun is hot, but I do not mind. It makes Damick's hair a pretty almond. I remember Andaraa saying she liked this about him, it is about the only thing I agree with her on. Though it is selfish of me to look on his appearance when he is grieving so. But I cannot help it. He is impossible to ignore just as it is now impossible to ignore the scent of blossoms in the spring,

    “It is coming,” He says.
Or to ignore the silver of a full moon on a clear night,
    “What, rain?” I ask.
Or to ignore the sound of water rushing down a fall, or, or --
    “Yes.” He replies.
Or to ignore the feel of the coming of rain.
    “How do you know?”
He is all of these, scent, sight, sound, feel...
    “I know it in my bones. I have rain in my bones you know.”
All except taste. But I know it will remain so.
    “That sounds very cold.”
And that is fine, I have sensed four fifths of the world through him.
    “It is.”
Even without taste or drink, I have thrived in those four fifths.
    “Are your bones made of rain or is the outside bone and the inside filled of rain?”
He has given me more than I could have found on my own.
    “The latter, I think. Although I have not checked.”

I had thought he might be making a joke about the rain in his bones, but his face remained expressionless. Perhaps he meant it was the sadness within him that could sense the coming rain. A single tear rolled down his face and at the same moment a small raindrop hit the dirt. I could hear the small pit that echoed upon its contact. I had begun to reach for his hand but something stopped me. He was not mine to love.

At that moment, I felt my physical senses leave me. Not in the same way that the fairy had taken them, instantly and cruelly. No, this was fading and dizzying, like I had the chance to reach out and pull them back if I tried. He was not mine to love; I would lose my senses forever.

The smell of the grass and rain fades and with it, the emeralds and cobalts and other nuances of color I had perhaps begun to take for granted. The sound of the pounding rain resigns to my imagination and so does the feel of Damick's hand. I see his mouth move and expect to hear nothing, but I am granted his last words to me.
