About Themis University

Themis University is a private university located on the island of Crete. Founded in 20XX as an institution dedicated to Justice in the Arts, Athletics and overall Academics, it is the oldest mythical university on the island. Sponsored by locals in an effort to unite the intellectual and physiological parties on the island, the university was named after the Titaness, Themis, the goddess of Divine Law. Originally, the institution was merely a gathering place for those wishing to reconcile preferred activities, but it soon blossomed into a conservatory for the athletic and artistic alike. The university sponsors additional fields of academic study as they are desired by the student body.
Themis University is organized into two constituent schools: the College of Athletics, and the College of Art. While both schools are managed under the university's Academic Order, each school's faculty oversees its respective curriculum and degree programs. In addition to a central campus in Heraklion, the university owns athletic facilities in Sitia and Ierapetra.
Regardless of college affiliation, all students have unbridled access to the Themis University Library, which serves all constituent schools, and holds more than 15 million volumes spanning numerous academic disciplines.
Themis University undergraduates follow a liberal arts curriculum. Almost all members of the Faculty of Arts and Athletics teach undergraduate courses, more than 2,000 of which are offered annually. Students compete intercollegiately as the Themis Myrmidons.

Who is Themis?

Themis is an ancient Greek Titaness. She is described as "[the Lady] of good counsel", and is the personification of divine order, fairness, law, natural law, and custom. Her symbols are the Scales of Justice, tools used to remain balanced and pragmatic. Themis, quite simply, means "divine law." To the ancient Greeks she was originally the organizer of the "communal affairs of humans." Moses Finley remarked of Themis:

"Themis is untranslatable. A gift of the gods and a mark of civilized existence, sometimes it means right custom, proper procedure, social order, and sometimes merely the will of the gods (as revealed by an omen, for example) with little of the idea of right."

At Themis University, we strive to be a mark of civilized existence as we unite the Divine Laws governing the Arts, Athletics and all Academics.
